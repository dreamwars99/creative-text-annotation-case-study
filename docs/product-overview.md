# PlotScope Product Overview

PlotScope is an AI-native Story OS for writers who need to organize complex fiction, scripts, game narratives, or serialized worlds.

The product starts from either an existing manuscript or a single idea, then helps the writer structure the work into reviewable story data: concept, characters, plot, world rules, draft text, and later publishing previews.

This public repository does not contain the private product source code. It only summarizes the problem, product direction, and sanitized evidence for review.

## Problem

Writers often manage story logic across documents, spreadsheets, notes, post-its, wiki pages, and memory. As a work grows, the hard part is no longer only writing sentences. It is remembering what is true, what changed, which character knows what, and how a new scene affects the rest of the story.

Large fictional worlds make this worse. Even famous authors have needed external help from dedicated readers or fandom knowledge to keep continuity straight. Independent writers, web novel writers, game writers, and small narrative teams usually do not have that support.

## Solution

PlotScope turns creative text into structured, reviewable story data.

The system is designed around three surfaces:

- World Atelier: organize the story world, concept, characters, and plot structure.
- Workspace: write, import, split, edit, preview, and continue the manuscript.
- Share Your World: later public reading and community surface.

AI is not treated as the owner of truth. It proposes candidates. The user reviews, edits, applies, or rejects them.

## Current MVP Focus

The current MVP focuses on:

- New project entry flow.
- Concept and core premise workflow.
- Character management and relationship map.
- Workspace writing desk and public preview controls.
- Local SLLM experiment for core premise extraction.
- AI-ready frontend contracts for future model routing.

## Public Preview Boundary

The public preview is intentionally limited:

- Live AI inference can be disabled.
- Mock or deterministic fallback suggestions may be shown.
- Private story ideas, unpublished source code, internal prompts, and provider settings are excluded.
